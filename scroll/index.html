The Cathode Ray Tube Television

Past and Present

1859

A German physicist was the first to theorize the existence of cathode rays. Plucker wrote about a mysterious fluorescent glow that could be created in a vacuum tube. The glow was found to be sensitive to electromagnetic fields.

1870

An English physicist designed an experimental cathode ray tube called the Crookes tube. Over the next decade, Crookes' design helped scientists study the properties of cathode rays. In 1895, a Crookes tube was used in the discovery of X-rays.

1938

Du Mont was a tinkerer and an entrepreneur. He's credited with inventing the first commercially viable cathode ray tube oscillograph. Du Mont's devices were both longer lasting, and easier to manufacture than other contemporary designs.

If you have an old CRT TV...

Maybe there's an art museum or a gaming association that could use it.

If not, disposal might take some planning...

Glass-encased vacuum tubes can be explosive, and older models may contain toxic substances. Your best bet is to seek out a certified CRT recycler.
